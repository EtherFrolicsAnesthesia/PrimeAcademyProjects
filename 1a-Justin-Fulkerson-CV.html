I relocated to Fort Lauderdale, Florida in September 2022. With this new location, I’m looking for
practice environments that value skilled and versatile anesthesia providers. My ideal practice is one that
provides variety, a friendly team structure, and appreciates the importance of work/life balance. I welcome new
professional challenges and case types.
What I Bring to the Healthcare Team:
Case Types and Complexity: I have a diverse background from both military and civilian practice, working
heavily with ASA 3-4 patients in independent settings. I have experience in both high-acuity tertiary hospitals
and the fast-paced realm of outpatient surgery centers. I also rather enjoy teaching (didactic or clinical) having
done so at the doctoral level across a variety of subjects. I am proficient in neuraxial, regional, or general
anesthesia. As a perioperative clinic director, I pride myself in providing thoughtful consults to aide in
optimizing each unique patient. I have a unique perspective here as I am both a nurse anesthetist and a nurse
practitioner. Lastly, I have a passion for humanitarian work. To say I’m flexible with the resources available is
an understatement. Whether it’s diagnosing and treating medical problems or managing perioperative
anesthesia care, I’m up for the task.
Embracing Change, Technology, and New Evidence: Staying up to date is essential to ensure high-quality
patient care. I maintain an RSS feed that automatically brings me new journal articles across the anesthesia,
critical care, and surgical literature. I disseminate high-yield information among professional communities,
colleagues, and my own practice. I am definitely technologically savvy, as evidenced by launching telehealth
perioperative services in the VA healthcare system. As a hobby, I teach myself coding and I’m currently
building the digital infrastructure for a nonprofit that I founded. Most recently, I have poured myself into
becoming a point of care ultrasound guru to aide in both the perioperative realm as well as in austere settings.
Leadership and Team Cohesion: Eleven years in the Navy afforded me an incredible amount of leadership
opportunities. This has translated well into teaching roles (University of Minnesota and The Army Graduate
Program in Anesthesia Nursing) as well as administrative (Memphis VA Perioperative Clinic Director.) Now I
am learning the business side of running a nonprofit and serving on the board. Spending time in traveling
anesthesia and humanitarian work means I have exposure to working with diverse teams just about anywhere in
the world. I strongly believe that at the core of quality patient care is ensuring cohesive teams. Lifting up
colleagues and learning as much as possible from each other is central to building this environment – we don’t
do this job alone.
Thank you for your time and I look forward to hearing more from you.
Very Respectfully,
Justin S. Fulkerson
